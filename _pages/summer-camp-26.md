# BSA Health Physical form [LINK](https://filestore.scouting.org/filestore/HealthSafety/pdf/680-001_ABC.pdf)

Parts A, B1, and B2 are completed, and an examining physician for all scouts signs Part C. Sports and other physicals can be used instead of Part C but will be accepted only if accompanied by parts A and B1, B2 of the Health Form. These need to be done ASAP and delivered to the Scoutmaster! Your scout will not be able to attend camp without a complete health form. The MU Quick Care at the Grindstone Hy-Vee provides camp physicals.

(a)	Scouts will be walking a lot during the day. A second pair, in case the first pair gets wet, is a good idea. 
(b)	Showers are available at camp, and scouts can shower daily if they desire
(c)	Scouts can sleep in the canvas wall tents provided, OR they can set up one of the troop tents. If they plan to stay in a wall tent, a cot is nice for sleeping. If they plan to sleep in a troop tent, then a sleeping pad will be used—the troop tents cannot fit cots.  
(d)	The trading post is open for set hours daily, and scouts can spend money there for items like juice, soda, candy, and camp souvenirs. Lake of the Ozarks Scout Reservation hats, T-shirts, and sweatshirts are available for purchase. We do not know the exact price, but $20-$40 will likely be sufficient for one of the items.

# Contacting your scout and camp

## Personal cell phones and camp

Troop policy limits the use of personal phones during campouts. If your scout brings a personal cell phone, that phone will be locked in the scoutmaster’s car. Scouts can access their phone to call their parents by asking an adult leader. The scoutmaster will be at camp all week and can be reached by call or text (515-520-0564) should you need to contact your scout. 

## Emergency Camp Contact

The main telephone in camp is for camp business and emergencies ONLY. The camp telephone is (573) 374-5761, and it is used for camp business and emergencies only. Telephone messages should be limited to emergencies only.
